Rebuilding politicians with neural networks

What happens when you train neural networks on Scott Morrison and Bill Shorten's language? Well it's scary how far you can get to replicating in just a couple of days.

Advance Party's crack at using neural networks to dissect the authenticity - or lack their of - coming from our political leaders was a mid-campaign thought.

We trained Cyberbill and RoboScoMo on more than 120,000 words spoken by their human counterparts. The results aren't perfect, but you can already see pet themes and phrases stasrting to pop up for both leaders. Since the accuracy of a neural network improves over time with more training and more data, you can start to get a taste of how accurate something like this could become.

You can try it out for yourself, by asking your own questions or picking samples from real campaign interviews. The answers won't fool anybody just yet, but you should see even with a very short amount of training, the models have started to pick up on key phrases and styles of each leader.
